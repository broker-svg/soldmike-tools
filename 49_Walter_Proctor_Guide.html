Listing Appointment Guide

49 Walter Proctor Rd · East Gwillimbury · Personalized to this seller situation · For your eyes only

⚑ Know Before You Walk In
These sellers bought at the absolute GTA peak — $1,027,000 in January 2022. They already tried to sell at $998,800 in 2024 and sat for 85 days with no deal. They know something is wrong. Your job is not to be the bearer of bad news — it's to be the first agent who actually explains it clearly and gives them a path forward. The number they need to hear is around $879,900. That conversation has to be built on trust first.

Pre-Arrival Prep
Before you leave

Don't wing this one. These sellers are sophisticated — they've been through this before and they know they're underwater. Walk in with everything loaded and ready.

  • ◆ This evaluation HTML open on your tablet or laptop — tabs 1–5 ready to present
  • ◆ Know the two comps cold: 17 Carratuck ($965K → adjusted $870K) and 24 Briarfield ($871,888, 7 DOM)
  • ◆ Know their ownership history by heart: bought $441K (2017) → $788K (2021) → $1,027,000 at peak (Jan 2022)
  • ◆ Know their 2024 terminated listing: $998,800, 85 DOM, no sale
  • ◆ Research current active competition — pull any live townhomes in Sharon Village before you go
  • ◆ Pre-listing package already sent? If not, send it now so they've seen your background before the door opens
  • ◆ Business cards, listing agreement, feature sheet template ready
  • ◆ Arrive 5 min early. Sit in the car. Take a breath. You have the data — trust it.
<\!-- PHASE 1: ARRIVAL & RAPPORT -->
1
Arrival & Rapport
First 5–10 min

You win or lose the listing before you open the laptop. Make them feel seen and heard — not sold to. Walk in warm, observe everything, compliment something specific (not generic).

When they open the door
Opening Line
"Hey, great to meet you — I appreciate you having me over. I've done a lot of work on this property ahead of time, so I want to make sure tonight is actually useful for you, not just a sales pitch."
Sets the tone immediately — you're here to work, not perform.
As they show you in — observe silently
  • ◆ Condition of the entryway, flooring, walls — original builder or upgraded?
  • ◆ Natural light — how does it feel vs. what a buyer will feel?
  • ◆ Any obvious repairs needed? (note mentally, bring up gently in tour)
  • ◆ Are they nervous? Defensive? Tired? Read the room.
Rapport openers — pick one, keep it genuine
"How long have you been in Sharon Village? Do you know the neighbourhood well — did you see a lot of the other streets when you were buying?"
"Rodeo Fine Homes built this one, right? Were you involved in the build process or did you purchase it finished?"
"Before we get into everything — can I grab a quick tour of the home first? I like to see it through a buyer's eyes before we sit down and talk numbers."
This buys you time, lets you observe, and makes the tour feel natural.
<\!-- PHASE 2: HOME TOUR -->
2
Home Tour — See It Like a Buyer
10–15 min

Walk every room. Don't rush. Ask questions that get them talking and reveal what they've done (or haven't done). What you learn here changes how you position the property and handle the condition conversation.

Main Floor
Kitchen
"Are these the original countertops and backsplash from the builder? They look clean — have they been well maintained?"
Note: Prior MLS listing confirmed granite countertops, builder-grade. No major kitchen upgrades identified. Confirms the base evaluation — no positive condition overlay needed unless they tell you otherwise.
Living Area
"The gas fireplace is a nice feature — does it get used? Buyers always love seeing that especially in townhomes."
The fireplace is actually a positive differentiator vs. 24 Briarfield which didn't have one. Remember this for the pricing conversation — it's a +$5K adjustment in the comps.
Upper Level
Primary Suite
"5-piece ensuite — is this original? Any updates to the bathrooms since you moved in?"
Secondary Rooms
"Are these being used as bedrooms right now, or has anything been set up differently? Staging-wise, I always like to think about what will feel most natural to a buyer walking through."
Basement
"So it's unfinished right now — any work started down here, or completely raw? Have you thought about whether to finish it before listing or leave it for the buyer?"
Important: The unfinished basement is baked into the evaluation. 17 Carratuck had a fully finished basement and that alone accounts for a $45K downward adjustment. If they've done any work down here, you need to know — it could change the overlay. If it's completely raw, leave the evaluation as-is.
Parking & Exterior
"3-car parking total with the single garage — that's solid for a townhome in this area. Is the garage fully functional, any shelving or storage built in?"
"The brick and stone exterior looks like it's held up well. Any maintenance done on the roof, HVAC, or major systems since 2017?"
Mechanical updates (furnace, A/C, roof) don't typically change the market value dramatically, but they give you ammunition for the listing description and buyer conversations.
<\!-- PHASE 3: DISCOVERY QUESTIONS -->
3
Sit Down — Discovery & Their Story
15–20 min

You're at the table now. Close the laptop for a few minutes. This phase is all about understanding their why, their timeline, their emotional state, and their expectations — before you show them a single number. The more they talk, the better you can frame everything that follows.

Why & Motivation
Question 1
"What's driving the decision to sell now? Is this more of a lifestyle change, a financial decision, or are you buying somewhere else?"
Listen for urgency. If they're buying elsewhere, they have a hard deadline. If it's financial, the loss conversation becomes even more delicate — but more necessary.
Question 2
"When you think about the ideal outcome here — is it a specific number you need, a specific timeline, or a combination of both?"
This separates price-focused sellers from timeline-focused ones. It changes your entire strategy and how you present the pricing tiers.
The 2024 Listing — Address It Directly
💡 Don't avoid the elephant in the room. They tried to sell in 2024 and it didn't work. Bring it up yourself — it builds massive trust. If you pretend it didn't happen, you've lost credibility before you've opened the dashboard.
Bring It Up
"I noticed from the data that there was a prior listing in 2024 — $998,800 and it came off market after about 85 days. Without getting into what happened with that agent, what was your experience? What did buyers say, or did you get any feedback at all?"
You already know the answer — it was overpriced for the market conditions. But let them tell their version. It reveals what they believe, how frustrated they are, and how ready they are for a different approach.
Follow Up
"And since then, has the market improved in your mind, stayed the same, or do you feel like it's gotten worse? What's your read on where Sharon Village townhomes are trading right now?"
This tells you how anchored they are to the $998,800 or $1,027,000 number in their head. You'll need this information when you deliver the pricing conversation.
Financial Situation (Handle with care)
Question
"I want to make sure I understand your situation fully — do you have flexibility in the number you walk away with, or are there financial targets that need to be hit for this move to make sense?"
You don't need to know their mortgage balance. But you need to know if they're handcuffed to a number. If they bought at $1,027,000 and owe close to that, pricing at $879,900 might not work for them — which is a conversation you need to have before you sign anything.
Buy/Sell Situation
Question
"Are you planning to buy something after this sells, or are you moving to something that's already lined up? And if you're buying — is the purchase conditional on this sale, or do you have flexibility there?"
Their Expectations of You
Last Discovery Question
"Before I show you everything I've put together — what does working with the right agent look like to you? What did the last experience teach you about what you want differently this time?"
This is gold. Whatever they say becomes your positioning for the rest of the night.
<\!-- PHASE 4: MARKETING -->
4
Marketing Presentation
10–12 min

Open the laptop. Pull up this evaluation. Start with the marketing — NOT the price. Sellers need to trust your process before they accept your number. Show them how you work first.

💡 Sequence matters. If you show the price first, they spend the entire marketing section calculating whether they agree with your number. Show marketing first — they're more receptive to the price when they understand what they're getting for it.
Open Tab 1 — Overview
"So this is what I built specifically for 49 Walter Proctor — let me start by walking you through what I found on the property itself, because the data I'm about to show you comes from MPAC, GeoWarehouse, and MLS. It's not opinion — it's public record."
→ Open Tab 1
Your Marketing Approach — Speak It in Your Own Words
  • ◆ Photography & Video: "The way buyers shop now is online first. The photos are the showing before the showing. I use professional photography, twilight shots where they work, and video walkthroughs — because the first 48 hours a listing is live is when you get the most traffic, and you need to capture that."
  • ◆ Social Media: "I run paid campaigns on Instagram and Facebook that target buyers actively searching in East Gwillimbury and surrounding areas. Your property gets exposure beyond just MLS."
  • ◆ Staging Consultation: "I'll walk through with you and give you specific recommendations on what to declutter, what to move, and how to set the space so a buyer sees the potential — not the furniture."
  • ◆ Open Houses: "I do strategic open houses — not just to get neighbours in, but because the buyers who show up on a Sunday are usually the ones without an agent who are still in early research mode. That's a conversion opportunity."
  • ◆ Agent Network: "I work the agent community directly — I let other buyer agents know what's coming to market before it hits, so on Day 1 we have pre-interested buyers, not just passive searchers."

The Hard Conversation — Their Purchase Price
Address it directly. Don't let them bring it up first.

We've seen a significant correction since then, and this property, based on what the market is actually doing right now, is worth approximately $870,000. That's not a reflection of the home — the home is in good shape. It's a reflection of what the entire market has done since 2022. I know that's a hard number to hear. But you already experienced what happens when you try to fight the market — 85 days, no sale. My job is to get you the best number the market will give you right now, get the deal done cleanly, and let you move forward. That's what I'm here to do."

<\!-- PHASE 6: CLOSE -->
6
The Close & Objection Handling
10–15 min

You've shown your work, you've been honest, you've built trust. Now it's time to ask for the business — directly. Don't hover around it. Don't oversell. Just ask.

The Ask
Closing Line
"Based on everything I've shown you tonight and everything you've told me — are you ready to move forward? I'd like to get this on market properly and get you the outcome you deserve. I'm ready to go whenever you are."
Common Objections
❝ We need at least $900,000 to make the numbers work ❞
"I hear you — and I respect that you need to know the outcome before you commit. Let me ask you this: if we list at $879,900 and we get an offer at $875,000, is that a deal you can do? Because that's what the market supports right now, and I'd rather have that conversation honestly now than have you go through another 85 days at a number buyers won't touch. What does walking away at $875K mean for your situation?"
❝ We want to wait until the market improves ❞
"That's a completely reasonable thought — and if your timeline allows for it, that's a valid strategy. What I'd caution is this: rates haven't given buyers significant relief, inventory is building, and we've already seen the market correct 6.9% year-over-year. I can't promise that waiting 6 months gets you more money — it might, or it might not. What I can control is getting you the best price in today's market with exceptional execution. How much time do you realistically have before you have to make a decision?"
❝ We're talking to a few other agents ❞
"Absolutely — you should. I'd actually encourage it. All I ask is that when you're comparing agents, you compare the data, not the price they tell you they can get you. Any agent can write a higher number on a piece of paper. The question is: which agent is going to give you an honest market read and execute flawlessly when the listing goes live? I think what I put in front of you tonight shows you how I work. I'll let you make the call."
❝ We think it's worth more because of what we paid ❞
"I completely understand — and that number is real, the pain of it is real. But buyers don't care what you paid. They care what the market is doing today. 24 Briarfield sold for $871,888 in 7 days in January. That's the same house, same street, same everything. The buyer who walks into your property is going to pull that comp on their phone. Our job is to be ahead of that conversation, not behind it."
If They're Not Ready to Sign Tonight
"That's completely fine — I don't want you to feel rushed. Here's what I'll do: I'll leave you with everything I showed you tonight. Take a couple of days. But I do want to be honest — if you decide to move forward, I'd like to get the marketing process started within the week so we hit the spring market at the right time. Can we agree to connect again by [specific day]?"
Always leave with a specific next step. "I'll call you" is not a next step.
If They Sign
"Perfect — I appreciate your trust and I'm going to take care of you. Here's what happens next: I'll get the listing setup checklist going tonight, we'll book photography within the next few days, and I'll have a launch plan to you by tomorrow. You made the right call."
