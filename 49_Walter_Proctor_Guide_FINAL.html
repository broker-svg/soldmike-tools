Phase 1
Arrival & Rapport
First 5-10 minutes · Win it before you open the laptop
You win or lose the listing before you open the laptop. Make them feel seen and heard — not sold to. Walk in warm, observe everything, compliment something specific.
Opening Line — When They Answer the Door
Say This
"Hey, great to meet you — I appreciate you having me over. I've done a lot of work on this property ahead of time, so I want to make sure tonight is actually useful for you, not just a sales pitch."
Sets the tone immediately — you're here to work, not perform.
Observe Silently As You Walk In
Condition of entryway, flooring, walls — original builder or upgraded?
Natural light — how does it feel vs. what a buyer will feel?
Any obvious repairs needed? Note mentally, bring up gently during the tour
Are they nervous? Defensive? Tired? Read the room before you say anything else
Rapport Openers — Pick One, Keep It Genuine
Option A — Neighbourhood
"How long have you been in Sharon Village? Do you know the neighbourhood well — did you see a lot of the other streets when you were buying?"
Option B — The Build
"Rodeo Fine Homes built this one, right? Were you involved in the build process or did you purchase it finished?"
Option C — Best One · Buys You Time & Gets You a Tour
"Before we get into everything — can I grab a quick tour of the home first? I like to see it through a buyer's eyes before we sit down and talk numbers."
This buys you time, lets you observe, and makes the tour feel natural. Use this one by default.
2
Phase 2
Home Tour
10-15 minutes · Walk every room · Don't rush
Ask questions that get them talking and reveal what they've done (or haven't done). What you learn here changes how you position the property.
Main Floor
Kitchen
Ask
"Are these the original countertops and backsplash from the builder? They look clean — have they been well maintained?"
Note: Prior MLS listing confirmed granite countertops, builder-grade. No major kitchen upgrades identified. Confirms base evaluation — no positive condition overlay unless they say otherwise.
Living Area & Fireplace
Ask
"The gas fireplace is a nice feature — does it get used? Buyers always love seeing that, especially in townhomes."
Differentiator: The fireplace is a positive vs. 24 Briarfield which didn't have one. This is a +$5K adjustment in the comps. Remember this for the pricing conversation.
Upper Level
Primary Suite
Ask
"5-piece ensuite — is this original? Any updates to the bathrooms since you moved in?"
Secondary Bedrooms
Ask
"Are these being used as bedrooms right now, or has anything been set up differently? Staging-wise, I always like to think about what will feel most natural to a buyer walking through."
Basement
Ask
"So it's unfinished right now — any work started down here, or completely raw? Have you thought about whether to finish it before listing or leave it for the buyer?"
Important: The unfinished basement is baked into the evaluation. 17 Carratuck had a fully finished basement — that alone accounts for a -$45K downward adjustment. If they've done any work down here, reassess the overlay.
Parking & Exterior
Parking
"3-car parking total with the single garage — that's solid for a townhome in this area. Is the garage fully functional, any shelving or storage built in?"
Exterior & Mechanicals
"The brick and stone exterior looks like it's held up well. Any maintenance done on the roof, HVAC, or major systems since 2017?"
Mechanical updates don't dramatically change market value but give ammunition for the listing description and buyer conversations.
3
Phase 3
Sit Down — Discovery
15-20 minutes · Close the laptop · Let them talk
This phase is all about understanding their why, timeline, emotional state, and expectations — before you show them a single number. The more they talk, the better you can frame everything that follows.
Why & Motivation
Question 1
"What's driving the decision to sell now? Is this more of a lifestyle change, a financial decision, or are you buying somewhere else?"
Listen for urgency. If they're buying elsewhere, they have a hard deadline. If it's financial, the loss conversation becomes even more delicate — but more necessary.
Question 2
"When you think about the ideal outcome here — is it a specific number you need, a specific timeline, or a combination of both?"
Separates price-focused sellers from timeline-focused ones. Changes your entire strategy and how you present the pricing tiers.
The 2024 Listing — Address It Directly
Don't avoid the elephant in the room

They tried to sell in 2024 and it didn't work. Bring it up yourself — it builds massive trust. If you pretend it didn't happen, you've lost credibility before you've opened the dashboard.

Bring It Up First
"I noticed from the data that there was a prior listing in 2024 — $998,800 and it came off market after about 85 days. Without getting into what happened with that agent, what was your experience? What did buyers say, or did you get any feedback at all?"
You already know the answer — it was overpriced. But let them tell their version. It reveals what they believe and how ready they are for a different approach.
Follow Up
"And since then, has the market improved in your mind, stayed the same, or do you feel like it's gotten worse? What's your read on where Sharon Village townhomes are trading right now?"
Tells you how anchored they are to the $998,800 or $1,027,000 number in their head. Critical before you deliver pricing.
Financial Situation — Handle with Care
Financial Question
"I want to make sure I understand your situation fully — do you have flexibility in the number you walk away with, or are there financial targets that need to be hit for this move to make sense?"
You don't need their mortgage balance. But you need to know if they're handcuffed to a number. If they owe close to $1,027,000, pricing at $879,900 might not work — have that conversation before you sign anything.
Buy / Sell Situation
Buy/Sell Question
"Are you planning to buy something after this sells, or are you moving to something that's already lined up? And if you're buying — is the purchase conditional on this sale, or do you have flexibility there?"
Their Expectations of You
Last Discovery Question — This Is Gold
"Before I show you everything I've put together — what does working with the right agent look like to you? What did the last experience teach you about what you want differently this time?"
Whatever they say becomes your positioning for the rest of the night. Listen carefully. Don't interrupt. Let them finish completely before you respond.

Marketing Presentation
10-12 minutes · Open the laptop now · Tab 1 first
Sequence matters. Show marketing before the price. If you lead with the number, they spend the entire marketing section calculating whether they agree with it. Show what they're getting first — they're far more receptive to the number after.

Opening Line for the Laptop

Your Marketing — Speak These in Your Own Words
Photography & Video

"The way buyers shop now is online first. The photos are the showing before the showing. I use professional photography, twilight shots where they work, and video walkthroughs — because the first 48 hours a listing is live is when you get the most traffic, and you need to capture that."

Social Media & Paid Ads

"I run paid campaigns on Instagram and Facebook targeting buyers actively searching in East Gwillimbury and surrounding areas. Your property gets exposure beyond just MLS."

Staging Consultation

"I'll walk through with you and give you specific recommendations on what to declutter, what to move, and how to set the space so a buyer sees the potential — not the furniture."

Open Houses

"I do strategic open houses — not just to get neighbours in, but because buyers who show up on a Sunday are usually ones without an agent who are still in early research mode. That's a conversion opportunity."

Agent Network

"I work the agent community directly — I let other buyer agents know what's coming to market before it hits, so on Day 1 we have pre-interested buyers, not just passive searchers."

The Hard Conversation — Say It Yourself
Script — The Purchase Price Conversation
"I want to address something directly because I think it needs to be said — you bought this property in January 2022 for $1,027,000. That was the peak of the GTA market. We've seen a significant correction since then, and this property, based on what the market is actually doing right now, is worth approximately $870,000. That's not a reflection of the home — the home is in good shape. It's a reflection of what the entire market has done since 2022. I know that's a hard number to hear. But you already experienced what happens when you try to fight the market — 85 days, no sale. My job is to get you the best number the market will give you right now, get the deal done cleanly, and let you move forward. That's what I'm here to do."

Phase 6
The Close & Objections
10-15 minutes · Ask directly · Don't hover
You've shown your work, you've been honest, you've built trust. Now ask for the business — directly. Don't oversell it. Just ask.
The Ask
Closing Line
"Based on everything I've shown you tonight and everything you've told me — are you ready to move forward? I'd like to get this on market properly and get you the outcome you deserve. I'm ready to go whenever you are."
Objection Handlers
We need at least $900,000 to make the numbers work
"I hear you — and I respect that you need to know the outcome before you commit. Let me ask you this: if we list at $879,900 and we get an offer at $875,000, is that a deal you can do? Because that's what the market supports right now, and I'd rather have that conversation honestly now than have you go through another 85 days at a number buyers won't touch. What does walking away at $875K mean for your situation?"
We want to wait until the market improves
"That's a completely reasonable thought — and if your timeline allows for it, that's a valid strategy. What I'd caution is this: rates haven't given buyers significant relief, inventory is building, and we've already seen the market correct 6.9% year-over-year. I can't promise that waiting 6 months gets you more money — it might, or it might not. What I can control is getting you the best price in today's market with exceptional execution. How much time do you realistically have before you have to make a decision?"
We're talking to a few other agents
"Absolutely — you should. I'd actually encourage it. All I ask is that when you're comparing agents, you compare the data, not the price they tell you they can get you. Any agent can write a higher number on a piece of paper. The question is: which agent is going to give you an honest market read and execute flawlessly when the listing goes live? I think what I put in front of you tonight shows you how I work. I'll let you make the call."
We think it's worth more because of what we paid
"I completely understand — and that number is real, the pain of it is real. But buyers don't care what you paid. They care what the market is doing today. 24 Briarfield sold for $871,888 in 7 days in January. That's the same house, same street, same everything. The buyer who walks into your property is going to pull that comp on their phone. Our job is to be ahead of that conversation, not behind it."
If They're Not Ready to Sign Tonight
Always Leave with a Specific Next Step
"That's completely fine — I don't want you to feel rushed. Here's what I'll do: I'll leave you with everything I showed you tonight. Take a couple of days. But I do want to be honest — if you decide to move forward, I'd like to get the marketing process started within the week so we hit the spring market at the right time. Can we agree to connect again by [specific day]?"
"I'll call you" is not a next step. Get a specific day before you leave.
