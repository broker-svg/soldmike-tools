The Argument
Why This Evaluation Is Right
Comp #1 — 47 Elizabeth Dr sold for $1,340,000

Almost identical square footage at 1,240 sq ft. But Elizabeth had oil heat, septic sewer, only one bathroom, a partially finished basement, and mostly original finishes. 116 Lynwood has gas heat, municipal sewers, two bathrooms, a fully finished basement with separate entrance, and has been upgraded throughout. Every measurable feature favours 116 Lynwood. If Elizabeth got $1.34M with those negatives, this property — which is better in every category — cannot be worth less.

Comp #2 — 13125 Hwy 27 sold for $1,350,000

Recently renovated and comparable in condition. But it sits directly on Highway 27 — a major arterial road. 116 Lynwood is on a quiet residential crescent in the heart of Nobleton. Location alone accounts for a meaningful premium. That property also sat 103 days before finding its buyer, which confirms the highway frontage was a deterrent.

The Condition Proof — 4 Hawman Ave sold for $1,150,000

This is the most powerful data point. Hawman is 513 sq ft LARGER than 116 Lynwood at 1,752 sq ft — yet it sold for nearly $200,000 LESS than the direct comps. Why? As-is condition and tenant occupancy. This proves that in Nobleton, condition is the single most impactful factor. An upgraded home doesn't just hold value — it commands a significant premium over larger homes in worse shape.

Bottom line: A 20% increase from the 2018 purchase price would put the value at $1,170,000. That number means this upgraded, move-in-ready bungalow with gas, sewers, and two bathrooms would be worth less than a larger house sold as-is in rough condition. The comparable sales data makes that argument impossible to support. The market is telling us the value is in the $1.375M–$1.425M range, and the evidence is clear.
