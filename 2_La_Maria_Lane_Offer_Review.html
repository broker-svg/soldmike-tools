Michael's Recommendation

After three rounds of negotiation, two strong offers remain on the table. Here's my assessment:

The Kayalica offer at $1,081,000 is the strongest overall. It's the highest price by $6,000, comes with a larger deposit ($40,000 vs. $30,000), and the buyers have shown consistent commitment — increasing their offer three times through the process. That kind of persistence signals serious motivation to close this deal.

The Soma offer at $1,075,000 is competitive, but the lower deposit and the fact that it's delivered within 2 business days (rather than upon acceptance) introduces a slight timing gap. They also request more chattels. The earlier closing date of May 28 could be a consideration if timing is important to you.

Both offers carry the same conditions (financing and inspection, 5 business days). The key differentiator is price, deposit strength, and the Kayalica buyers' demonstrated willingness to negotiate upward three times.

My suggestion: the Kayalica offer gives you the strongest position. That said, this is your home and your decision — I'm here to make sure you have all the information you need.
