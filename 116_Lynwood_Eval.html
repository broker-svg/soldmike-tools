Market Trend Summary

The King Township / Nobleton detached market in Q1 2026 is firmly in buyer-leaning territory, though the March data is showing the first genuine stabilization signal. GTA detached homes averaged $1,342,375 in March 2026, down 6.4% year-over-year according to TRREB. Nobleton specifically is running at a 90.5% SP/LP ratio and 63-day average DOM — buyers are regularly getting 9–10% below asking, and homes are taking over two months to sell on average.

The wide spread between average and median DOM is the key tell: stale, overpriced listings are dragging up the average while correctly priced homes are moving faster. New listings in King Township are up approximately 37% year-over-year, giving buyers more choice and negotiating leverage. The months of inventory figure has tightened from an estimated 9.3 in January to approximately 4.7 in March — still elevated but moving in the right direction as spring activity builds.

Market classification: Buyer-leaning, early stabilization. Pricing strategy implication: the first two to three weeks of showings determine everything. Price right and sell quickly; let the home sit and it becomes a data point that drags down the next evaluation.
